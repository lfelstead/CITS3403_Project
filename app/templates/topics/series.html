Resistors connected one after the other are connected in series. This looks like:


Calculating the overall resistance of circuits only in series is easy! All you need to do is add up all the individual resistances using the formula:

RTotal = R1 + R2 + R3 + ...


This means that for this circuit:


The resistance is:

100Ω + 200Ω + 500Ω = 800Ω

As long as the final resistance is the same, the resistors can be arranged f in a variety of ways, whilst still giving the same result.
